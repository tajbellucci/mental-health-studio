Leaving Wix for WordPress: a therapist's migration guide

Wix makes a fine first website and a frustrating fifth year. If your practice has grown past drag-and-drop, this is the honest guide to moving — including the one big Wix-specific trap.

The Wix-specific problem: no content export

Unlike Squarespace, Wix offers no meaningful export. Your pages, posts and images must be moved by hand or with third-party tools. For a typical therapy site — five to fifteen pages — a careful manual migration is genuinely the cleanest path, and it doubles as a content audit: you'll find pages worth cutting, merging and rewriting.

Watch the URL structure

Wix generates its own URL patterns (blog posts often live under /post/…). WordPress defaults differ. Every migrated page needs its redirect mapped individually — this is tedious and completely non-optional if you rank for anything. Export your ranking pages from Search Console first so you know which URLs carry weight.

What you gain on the other side

  • Compliant intake: BAA-covered form plugins and integrations Wix can't match.
  • Real local SEO: full control of schema markup, titles, site architecture and speed.
  • EHR-adjacent integrations: booking widgets from SimplePractice-class tools embed cleanly.
  • Ownership: your site becomes portable property, not a subscription hostage.

The migration sequence

  1. Inventory ranking URLs in Search Console.
  2. Build the complete WordPress site at a staging address.
  3. Manually migrate and improve content, page by page.
  4. Rebuild forms on BAA-covered infrastructure.
  5. Write the redirect map, old URL to new, one-to-one.
  6. Switch DNS on a quiet weekday morning; keep Wix live as fallback for 30 days.
  7. Verify redirects, forms, booking and mobile rendering; submit the new sitemap.

Should you hire this out?

A confident DIYer can do this in a few focused weekends. Hire it out if any of these are true: you rank for terms that drive real inquiries (the redirect map has to be perfect), your forms touch client information (compliance isn't a weekend skill), or your time is worth more seeing clients than fighting DNS settings.

Bottom line: Wix to WordPress is the most manual of the platform migrations, but it's a one-time cost that buys permanent ownership and room to grow. Done in the right order, nothing breaks — and your next redesign will never require a migration again.
