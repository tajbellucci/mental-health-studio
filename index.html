FAQ

Fair questions, straight answers.

Why choose a mental-health-only agency over a general web agency?

Because the failure modes are different here. A generic agency can make a pretty site; it usually can't tell you which intake form setup leaks PHI, why therapy-seekers bounce from stocky "smiling couple" imagery, or how directory profiles and condition-based search actually drive caseloads. Specialization means we've already made the mistakes elsewhere — on our own time, not yours.

What does "HIPAA-compliant website or app" actually mean?

It means the parts of your digital presence that touch protected health information are configured to handle it lawfully: BAA-covered form, booking and hosting providers where PHI flows; encryption in transit and at rest; no PHI in analytics or ad pixels; and access controls on anything client-facing. We document these decisions per project, and we design so your website itself holds as little client data as possible. (UK and Canadian practices get the equivalent UK-GDPR / PIPEDA treatment.)

How long does a website or app take?

Websites: typically two weeks from audit to launch for a solo practice, longer for group practices and treatment centers with multiple programs. Mobile apps: scoped case by case — a booking-and-reminders app is weeks, not months; custom teletherapy features take longer. You get a timeline in the strategy stage, in writing.

Do you lock clients into contracts?

No. Ongoing services are month to month, and you own your domain, your site, your content and your data. If you leave, we hand everything over cleanly. Retention should come from results, not contracts.

Can you work with my existing website or EHR?

Usually, yes. We regularly work alongside SimplePractice, TherapyNotes, Alma, Headway and similar platforms, and we can rebuild, migrate or extend an existing site — including moving off Squarespace or Wix without losing your search rankings.

What does the free audit include, and what's the catch?

Fifteen checks across your website, search visibility, booking flow and compliance basics, written up and walked through on a short call. No catch: some practices hire us afterward, some fix things themselves with the report. Both outcomes are fine — the audit is how we'd want to be sold to.
